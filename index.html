Books you must read

  1. Rich Dad Poor Dad

    • Book by Robert Kiyosaki
      • The book advocates the importance of financial literacy (financial education), financial independence and building wealth through investing in assets, real estate investing, starting and owning businesses, as well as increasing one's financial intelligence (financial IQ).

  2. Atomic Habits: An Easy & Proven Way to Build Good Habits & Break Bad Ones

    • Book by James Clear
      • Atomic Habits deals with regular practices or routines that is not only small and easy to do but is also the source of incredible power; a component of the system of compound growth.

  3. The Psychology of Money: Timeless Lessons on Wealth, Greed, and Happiness.

    • Book by Morgan Housel.
      • The Psychology of Money' is an essential read for anyone interested in being better with money.

  4. Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind.

    • Book by Yuval Noah Harari
      • Sapiens is a great book that takes you through the journey of evolution of the human race since the ages. It explains evolution in such a unique and interesting manner than it keeps you engaged throughout the book.

My Finacial Advices

  1. Invest early as possible

    • With early age investments, you develop a habit of saving more. The more you invest, the more you get in future. Also 8th wonder of the world compounding wil take its time.

  2. Build an Emergency Fund.

    • You should create an emergency fund: Creating a safety net in job loss or uncertainty is always essential. Your emergency fund to cover at least 3 to 6 months' worth of living expenses.

  3. Fuck your greed

    • Learning and continuing to learn is the first step towards increasing wealth. So learn first than invest.

  4. Have a Budget & Know Your Cash-Flows.

    • he cash flow budget is a plan of how cash will be coming into the operation (cash inflows) and leaving the operation (cash outflows). The keyword is “cash.”
